If you live in Toronto, you know that the city is home to some of the best Caribbean food outside of the islands. Whether you are craving a heavy Friday team lunch or need serious takeout to bring back to your condo, Toronto's jerk chicken scene consistently delivers bold flavors and massive portions.

Here is a spotlight on Toronto's reigning champion of jerk, plus five other local legends you need to try.

### The Headliner: The Real Jerk (East End)
Located at 842 Gerrard Street East, The Real Jerk has been serving up deep, comforting Caribbean cuisine for over three decades. It perfectly blends a laid-back island atmosphere with the hustle of a busy Toronto diner. 

**What to Order for Lunch:**
* **The Classic Jerk Chicken Meal:** Marinated to the bone with their signature fiery spice blend and slow-cooked to perfection. It’s served alongside a generous mountain of rice and peas and creamy coleslaw. 
* **Curry Goat Roti:** If you want something wrapped up and incredibly filling, their rotis are legendary. The curry goat is tender, falling apart instantly.
* **Jerk Pork:** Often overshadowed by the chicken, the jerk pork is fattier, intensely smoky, and absolutely melts in your mouth.

### 5 More Toronto Jerk Legends to Try

#### 1. Chris Jerk Caribbean Bistro (Scarborough)
**The Vibe:** A legendary Scarborough takeout counter that commands massive lines every single day of the week. 

**What to Order:** The Jerk Chicken Shawarma Poutine. They took two iconic Toronto takeout foods and combined them into a masterpiece. If you want something traditional, their Jerk Chicken Shawarma wrap is unrivaled.

#### 2. Allwyn's Bakery (North York & Queen West)
**The Vibe:** The ultimate grab-and-go Jamaican bakery that has achieved cult status among Toronto students and office workers alike.

**What to Order:** The Jerk Chicken Stuffed Patty. They take a perfectly flaky beef patty, slice it open, and stuff it with jerk chicken and creamy coleslaw. It is the ultimate handheld lunch under $10. 

#### 3. Roywoods (CityPlace & Union Station)
**The Vibe:** Modern, fast, and incredibly convenient for the downtown crowd. It brings authentic flavors right to the base of the condo corridors.

**What to Order:** The classic Jerk Chicken Plate or their massive Jerk Chicken Sandwich on coco bread. 

#### 4. Rasta Pasta (Kensington Market)
**The Vibe:** A tiny, bustling fusion takeout window located right in the heart of eclectic Kensington Market. You'll smell the oil drum smokers from a block away.

**What to Order:** The "Vatican City" (jerk chicken on a panini) or the classic Jerk Chicken meal. If you are feeling adventurous, try their Jamaican-Italian fusion dishes like the "Dreadlock Pasta."

#### 5. Albert's Real Jamaican Foods (St. Clair West)
**The Vibe:** An old-school, no-frills neighborhood staple that has been anchoring the St. Clair West strip for years. 

**What to Order:** The Oxtail Stew or a large Jerk Chicken dinner. The gravy at Albert's is legendary—make sure to ask for extra gravy on your rice and peas!
