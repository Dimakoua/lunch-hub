While Downtown Toronto often grabs the headlines, Midtown is home to some of the city's most beloved and sophisticated dining spots. Spanning from Yonge and Eglinton to the charming streets of Davisville and Mount Pleasant, Midtown offers a mix of cozy neighborhood favorites and upscale culinary destinations.

## 1. Scaramouche - A Skyline Classic
Tucked away in a residential building near St. Clair, Scaramouche offers one of the most romantic views of the Toronto skyline. Known for its impeccable service and contemporary French-inspired menu, it's a perfect spot for celebrations. Don't leave without trying their legendary Coconut Cream Pie.

## 2. Stock Bar - A Multi-Storey Experience
Located in the historic Postal Station K building at Yonge and Eglinton, Stock Bar is a collaboration between Terroni and Cumbrae's. It features a sprawling rooftop patio, a sophisticated dining room, and a ground-floor market. It's the place to go for high-quality steaks and authentic Italian pasta in the heart of Midtown.

## 3. Piano Piano - Playful Italian
With its vibrant floral exterior and whimsical interior, Piano Piano brings a sense of fun to Midtown dining. Chef Victor Barry serves up "soulful" Italian food, including wood-fired pizzas and massive veal parmigiana. It’s a favorite for families and groups looking for a lively atmosphere.

## 4. Tabülè Middle Eastern Cuisine - Davisville Gem
For some of the best Middle Eastern food in the city, head to Tabülè on Bayview. Their fresh hummus, fluffy pita, and expertly grilled kebabs have made them a staple in the Davisville community. The warm, inviting atmosphere makes it a great choice for both lunch and dinner.

## 5. Byblos Uptown - Eastern Mediterranean Flavors
Byblos Uptown brings the coastal flavors of the Eastern Mediterranean to North Toronto. The menu is designed for sharing, featuring aromatic rice dishes, delicate dumplings, and wood-fired meats. The stunning, high-ceilinged space on Yonge Street is an experience in itself.
